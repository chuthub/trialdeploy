I, the road
      in long of wander, cede
      remit, my friend,
      decipher restless weary yarns
      from ever-farther worlds away.
    
        I, the road
        in kingdom vast and earthly
        tried and tested,
        stood in sun-blanched scenes of time,
        unshackled,
        seen as ashen memories.
    
        I, the road
          in places awful,
          hawkish, hostile, shadowed in and shuttered
          in the mornings, littered wretched,
          ghostly with disquietude.
    
        I, the road
        in dazzling splendor,
        gleaming, glinting,
        calling out to sight in beauty,
        visage to adorn this lonesome throw.
